Tracking sea level rise policies and impacts

Global sea level is rising at an increased rate and will continue to rise during this century. Regional factors such as ocean circulation, melting of ice sheets, and tectonic plate movement help determine the rate of sea level rise (SLR) which is predicted to be greater than the global average along some parts of the West Coast. Natural flooding, erosion, and storm event patterns are likely to be exacerbated by sea level rise, leading to significant social, environmental, and economic impacts.

Which jurisdictions are incorporating SLR into their Local Coastal Programs in California?

Local Coastal Programs (LCPs) are basic planning tools used by local governments to guide development in the coastal zone. LCPs contain the ground rules for future development, protection of coastal resources, and adaptation measures for SLR in California's 76 coastal cities and counties. Currently, no local jurisdictions substantially address SLR in their LCPs but that is about to change. The Ocean Protection Council and Coastal Commission are partnering with local jurisdictions to fund SLR planning along much of the coast to compliment other efforts under way including the development of Local Hazard Mitigation Plans and SLR Vulnerability Assessments (not displayed).

The West Coast Ocean Data Portal includes data viewing tools that help the state's coastal programs identify which jurisdictions are currently planning for SLR. These tools can help communicate ongoing work and highlight areas for future investment to protect our coastal communities.
