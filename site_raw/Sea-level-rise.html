Tracking sea level rise policies and impacts

Global sea level is rising at an increased rate and will continue to rise during this century. Regional factors such as ocean circulation, melting of ice sheets, and tectonic plate movement help determine the rate of sea level rise (SLR) which is predicted to be greater than the global average along some parts of the West Coast. When combined with extreme storm and tide events, SLR will negatively impact coastal ecosystems, coastal development, and public access.

Which local jurisdictions are planning for SLR in CA?

In California, no local jurisdictions substantially address SLR but that is about to change. The Ocean Protection Council and Coastal Commission are partnering with local jurisdictions to fund SLR planning along much of the coast. You can explore where this planning is taking place.

The West Coast Ocean includes data viewing tools that help the states coastal programs identify which jurisdictions are currently planning for SLR and which jurisdictions are most at risk. These tools can help communicate ongoing work and highlight areas for future investment to protect our coastal communities.
